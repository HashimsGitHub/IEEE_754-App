Solar Battery Household Model

Mathematical Modeling of Household Adoption Over Time

Problem Definition

We can model the number of households with solar batteries as a stock (or state variable) that changes over time. The inflow is the rate of new installations, and the outflow is the rate of removals.

Initial Stock (at t=0)
S(0) = 20 houses
Constant Inflow Rate
I = 6 houses per month
Constant Outflow Rate
O = 4 houses per month
Time Period
t = 12 months

Integral Equation Formulation

The stock at any future time t, S(t), is equal to the initial stock plus the integral of the net flow from time 0 to time t.

The net flow is: Inflow - Outflow = 6 - 4 = 2 houses per month.

S(t) = S(0) + ∫0t (I - O) dτ

Substituting the known values:

S(t) = 20 + ∫0t (6 - 4) dτ
S(t) = 20 + ∫0t 2 dτ

Solving the Integral Equation

We now solve for the stock S(t) at any time t.

S(t) = 20 + ∫0t 2 dτ
S(t) = 20 + [2τ]0t
S(t) = 20 + (2t - 2·0)
S(t) = 20 + 2t

This is the solution to our integral equation, giving us the number of households at any time t.

Final Value after 12 Months

To find the stock after 12 months, we substitute t = 12 into our solution S(t).

S(12) = 20 + 2 × 12
S(12) = 20 + 24
S(12) = 44

The final value of the stock after 12 months is

44 households
